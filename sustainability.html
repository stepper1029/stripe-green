Personal Sustainability

We need large-scale change to truly combat the climate crisis, but people should still be making every attempt to decrease their personal waste. If you have the means to make small changes in your lifestyle, you can begin to live more sustainably. People are constantly producing waste which can easily be eliminated.

What is sustainability?

In the context of the environmental movement, sustainability is living in a way that can be maintained for a long time without creating as much waste as you might normally. Living sustainable might seem like an overwhelming task. However, just a few extra steps in your life can make a big difference.

How can I become more sustainable?

Transportation

People use motor transportation almost everyday. There are a couple of easy things you can do when getting from place to place to decrease your CO2 emissions. This is especially important because The average US household produces 7.5 tons of CO2 equivalents per year.

  • Walk or bike when possible
  • Carpool
  • Take public transportation
  • Limit far travel when possible

Food Consumption

The food you eat makes more of an impact than you might think. It is always a good idea to cultivate your own food if you have the means to do so and shop for locally sourced foods.

  • Grow what you can in a home garden
  • Substitute single-use plastic for reusable containers, straws and water bottles from companies listed here
  • Cut meat out of your diet - It requires more than 4,000 gallons of water per day to produce food for a typical meat-eater but only 300 gallons of water to produce food for a typical vegan. Also, You can reduce your foodprint by a quarter just by cutting down on red meats such as beef and lamb.
